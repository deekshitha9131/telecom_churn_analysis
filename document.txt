Telecom Customer Churn Prediction & Revenue Risk Intelligence

1. Business Problem Definition
## What Telecom Churn Is
Customer churn refers to **customers discontinuing their telecom subscription services**, such as:
* mobile plans
* broadband services
* bundled packages

In subscription industries like telecom, churn typically occurs when a customer:
* cancels a contract
* switches to a competitor
* stops renewing services

A churn event results in **immediate loss of recurring revenue**.
## Why Churn is Dangerous for Telecom Companies
Telecom businesses rely heavily on **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)**.
When churn increases:
1. **Revenue drops**
2. **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) increases**
3. **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) decreases**
4. **Network planning becomes unstable**
5. **Investor confidence drops**

Telecom is extremely competitive, so even **small increases in churn can significantly reduce profitability**.
Example:
If a telecom company has:
* 1 million customers
* $60 average monthly revenue
Then:
1% churn increase = 10,000 customers lost
Revenue loss = $600,000 per month

## Business Impact of Customer Churn
Churn affects:
-> Revenue
Loss of subscription income.
->Marketing Cost
New customers must be acquired to replace lost ones.
->Operational Planning

Network capacity planning becomes harder.

### Competitive Position

High churn signals **customer dissatisfaction**.

---

## Business Questions This Project Should Answer

1. What percentage of customers churn?
2. Which customer segments have the highest churn risk?
3. What services or plans are associated with churn?
4. Which high-value customers are at risk?
5. How much **revenue is at risk due to churn?**
6. Which retention strategies could reduce churn?

# 5. Data Preparation Strategy

## Handling Missing Values

Typical steps:

* identify missing values
* remove records with critical missing fields
* impute non-critical values

Example:

```
TotalCharges may contain blank values.
Convert to numeric and handle missing rows.
```

---

## Handling Categorical Variables

Most telecom features are categorical.

Examples:

* contract type
* internet service
* payment method

---

## Encoding Techniques

Use:

* **One-hot encoding** for nominal variables
* **Label encoding** for binary variables

Example:

```
Contract → Month-to-month, One-year, Two-year
```

---

## Feature Engineering Ideas

Create additional features such as:

* tenure groups
* service count
* monthly charge categories
* risk indicators

---

## Outlier Treatment

Use:

* IQR method
* log transformation

for skewed features like:

* monthly charges
* total charges

---

## Data Splitting Strategy

Typical split:

```
Train set: 70%
Test set: 30%
```

Use **stratified sampling** because churn is imbalanced.

# 9. Evaluation Metrics

Churn prediction requires multiple metrics.

---

## Accuracy

Overall prediction correctness.

But **not reliable with imbalanced data**.

---

## Precision

How many predicted churn customers actually churn.

---

## Recall

How many real churn customers were identified.

---

## F1 Score

Balance between precision and recall.

---

## ROC-AUC

Measures classification ability across thresholds.

---

## Why Recall is Important

Missing a churn customer means:

```
lost customer
lost revenue
```

Therefore:

High **recall** ensures most churn customers are identified.
